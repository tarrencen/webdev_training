The Best Movies Ever According to Tarrence


My top 3 movies of all time.


Upgrade

The relevance of this movie only increases by the day. In spite of some truly gruesome violence,
Upgrade proves to be far greater than the sum of its parts. Set in a very near future, it depicts a world in which
players in the world of AI and cybernetic modification dominate society and virtually run rough-shod all over any
semblance of human legal or moral authority with the aim of establishing complete dominance over all realms of human
knowledge and ability. Its ending is the most thought-provoking commentary on consciousness I have been witness to in
any art medium.

Tombstone

While far from a perfect movie, the biggest thing Tombstone has going for it is a truly
show-stealing performance from the recently deceased Val Kilmer as Doc Holliday. I consider
it a treatise on what true friendship means.

Sinners

Director Ryan Coogler has achieved something singular with his most recent oeuvre, Sinners.
The movie engages every sensory mode available to it on the way to communicating the Black American
experience in a more complete way than has ever been done before. It is so densely impregnated with
gems and Easter eggs of cultural reference in its entirety, in every frame, that it has inspired
a popular tidal wave not just digital content creator analysis of its abundant instances of symbolism.
Sinners does all this and more while simultanadvancing the state of the art cinematographically.
