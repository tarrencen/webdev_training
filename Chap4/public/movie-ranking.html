The Best Movies Ever According to Tarrence


My top 3 movies of all time... right now


Upgrade

The relevance of this movie only increases by the day. In spite of some truly gruesome violence, Upgrade
proves to be far greater than the sum of its parts. Set in a very near future, it depicts a world in
which players in the AI and cyborg spheres of influence dominate society and virtually run rough-shod all over
any semblance of human legal or moral authority with the aim of establishing complete dominance over all realms
of human knowledge and ability. Its ending is, frankly, the most thought-provoking commentary on consciousness I
have been witness to in any art medium.

Tombstone

While far from a perfect movie, the biggest thing Tombstone has going for it is a truly
show-stealing performance from the recently deceased Val Kilmer as Doc Holliday. I consider
it a treatise on the true meaning of friendship.

Sinners

Director Ryan Coogler has achieved something singular with his most recent oeuvre, Sinners.
The movie engages every sensory mode available to it on the way to communicating the Black American
experience in a more complete way than has ever been done before. It is so densely impregnated with
gems and Easter eggs of cultural reference in its entirety, in every frame, that it has inspired
a popular tidal wave of digital content creator analysis of its abundant instances of symbolism.
Sinners does all this and more while simultaneously advancing the state of the art cinematographically.

Honorable mention: The Dark Knight

Christopher Nolan is one of my favorite directors. His cinematographic sense and the epic scale he seems to
bring are a few of the many reasons I love his catalog. His foray into the comic book superhero genre with the
Dark Knight trilogy may have been the best ever by a single director, though perhaps it's a low bar when the whole
genre is considered. The titular second iteration of the series, The Dark Knight, has another one of my favorite
performances by an actor, that of Heath Ledger as the Joker. It rightfully earned him a posthumous Oscar. Ledger
unarguably steals the show from the very beginning of the movie. Honestly, I could easily interchange this pick
with the above mentioned Tombstone.
